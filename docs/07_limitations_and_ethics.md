# 07 - Limitations and Ethics

## Evidence limitations

- No internal BSI documents, logs, tickets, policies, contracts, or workpapers were used.
- The source set does not include a public final forensic report.
- Service-recovery statements are not equivalent to independent uptime measurements.
- The audited financial statement contains management representations but is not a published cyber-forensic audit opinion.
- Reuters is used to attribute an external claim, not verify stolen-data content.

## Ethical boundaries

- No alleged leaked data was accessed, downloaded, inspected, or redistributed.
- No BSI system was scanned, tested, or contacted.
- No employee or customer personal information is stored.
- No logos, stock incident imagery, AI-generated illustration, dashboard, or infographic is used.
- Recruiter-facing outputs use an audit workpaper and a black-and-white memorandum.

## Wording guardrails

Do not write:

- "BSI controls failed."
- "LockBit definitely stole 15 million records."
- "The attack entered through phishing."
- "The forensic audit proved the root cause."

Use:

- "The reviewed public evidence is insufficient to assess control effectiveness."
- "Reuters reported LockBit's claim; the reviewed primary sources do not publish a verified affected-record inventory."
- "The initial access vector is not publicly observable in the reviewed sources."
- "OJK confirmed that forensic work was ongoing as of 13 May 2023."

## Date boundary

The research cut-off is 13 July 2026. Later disclosures may change the public evidence set and should be added as new source records rather than silently rewriting historical evidence.
